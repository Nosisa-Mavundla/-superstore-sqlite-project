<h1>Executive Summary</h1>
<p>Regional sales analysis identified the South region as the lowest-performing region in terms of total sales. To determine the underlying causes of this performance gap, a diagnostic analysis was conducted focusing on three potential drivers: order volume, customer spending behavior, and category performance. The analysis aims to identify whether South's underperformance is driven by lower demand, lower revenue per transaction, or weaknesses within specific product categories.</p>

<h1>Diagnostic Analysis</h1>
<h2>Hypothesis 1: South Region is generating fewest orders compared to other Regions.</h2>
<h3>Analysis</h3>
<p>Total unique orders were compared across all regions to determine whether differences in order volume contribute to South's lower sales performance.</p>
<h3>Finding</h3>
<p>The analysis revealed that the South region generated the lowest number of unique orders among all regions.</P>
<h3>Conclusion</h3>
<p>The hypothesis is supported. South's underperformance is partially driven by lower order volume, indicating weaker customer demand relative to other regions. Since the region generates fewer transactions than its counterparts, it has fewer opportunities to generate revenue, contributing to its lower overall sales performance.</p>

<h2>Hyphothesis 2: Customers in South Region spend less per order</h2>
<h3>Analysis</h3>
<p> Compare Average Order Value among all regions.</P>
<h3>Finding</h3>
<p>South recorded the highest Average Order Value among the regions, outperforming both Central and West.</p>
<h3>Conclusion</h3>
<p>The hypothesis was not supported by the data. Although South generated the lowest total sales, customers in the region spend more per transaction than customers in several higher-performing regions. This indicates that lower customer spending is not the cause of South's underperformance. The findings suggest that the sales gap is more likely driven by lower order volume rather than lower transaction value.</p>

<h2>Hyphothesis 3: Category performance is driving the sales gap.</h2>
<h3>Analysis</h3>
<p>Sales were analyzed by product category within each region to identify category-level differences and determine whether specific categories contribute to South's lower sales performance.</P>
<h3>Finding</h3>
<p>South generated the lowest sales across all three product categories: Furniture, Office Supplies, and Technology. While Technology remained the region's highest-performing category, its sales were substantially lower than those recorded in East and West. Similar performance gaps were observed in Furniture and Office Supplies.</p>
<h3>Conclusion</h3>
<p>The hypothesis is supported. Category-level performance contributes to South's underperformance, as the region consistently generates lower sales across all major product categories. The findings suggest that South's sales gap is not driven by a single weak category but rather by broadly lower category performance across the product portfolio.</p>
